# Mechanics

How markets work, supply/demand, and liquidity. This page covers the structural mechanics underlying financial markets: how prices are discovered, what drives order flow, and how liquidity conditions affect execution and volatility.

## Primary vs. Secondary Markets

- The **primary market** is where securities are created and sold for the first time
  - A company sells new stocks or bonds directly, for example through an **IPO** (Initial Public Offering)
  - In an IPO, an underwriting firm determines the offer price based on internal research not disclosed to the public
- The **secondary market** is where those securities are subsequently traded among investors
  - This is what is commonly called the "Stock Market"
  - It includes exchanges like NASDAQ and the New York Stock Exchange (NYSE)

## Orders

Brokers receive instructions from clients in the form of **orders**:

- **Limit Order** — buy or sell a security at a specified price or better
- **Market Order** — buy or sell a security immediately at whatever price is available

### Order Book

The **Order Book** is the live record of all outstanding limit orders, mapping buyers to sellers:

- **Bid** — the highest price a buyer is willing to pay
- **Ask** — the lowest price a seller is willing to accept

| Buyers | Sellers |
| --- | --- |
| $27 · 500 shares | $31 · 500 shares |
|  | $32 · 1,000 shares |

- **Last Price / Last Trade** — the price of the most recently filled order (limit or market). This is the value shown in stock price charts.

## Candlestick Charts

Candlestick charts visualise the price activity of a security within a given period. Each candle encodes four data points: **Open**, **Close**, **High**, and **Low**.

- Green candle → price closed higher than it opened (up day)
- Red candle → price closed lower than it opened (down day)

![Example candlestick chart](/fundamentals/candlestick-chart.png)

The **body** of the candle spans the open-to-close range. The **wicks** (shadows) extend to the intraday high and low.

![Candlestick anatomy: High, Open, Close, Low, upper and lower shadow](/fundamentals/candlestick-anatomy.png)

- **High** — highest price reached during the period
- **Low** — lowest price reached during the period
- **Open** — price at the start of the period
- **Close** — price at the end of the period

The **volume bars** at the bottom show the number of shares traded: green on up days, red on down days.
