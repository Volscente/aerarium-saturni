# Fixed Income

Bonds, treasury bills, and interest rate sensitivity. Fixed-income instruments are debt contracts that pay a defined stream of cash flows. This page explains how bonds are priced, how interest rate changes affect them, and their role in capital preservation.

## Topics

- Bond anatomy: coupon, maturity, and face value
- Yield to maturity (YTM) and current yield
- The price-yield inverse relationship
- Duration and convexity as interest rate risk measures
- Treasury bills, notes, bonds, and TIPS
- Credit risk and rating agency frameworks
