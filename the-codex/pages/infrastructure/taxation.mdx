# Taxation

Capital gains, dividends, and local tax efficiency. Tax drag is one of the largest and most controllable costs for an investor. This page covers how different types of investment income are taxed and strategies for minimizing the burden legally.

## Germany

### Sparer-Pauschbetrag (Savings Allowance)

Germany provides an annual tax-free allowance on investment income — the **Sparer-Pauschbetrag**. The current allowance is **€1,000 per person** (€2,000 for couples filing jointly). Income from investments up to this threshold is exempt from capital gains tax (_Abgeltungsteuer_).

This allowance has grown over time (it was €801 in 2020). Maximising its use each year — by realising just enough gains or receiving just enough dividends to fill the allowance without exceeding it — is a meaningful tax optimisation for smaller portfolios.

### Teilfreistellungsquote (Partial Exemption)

For equity funds (including ETFs with at least 51% equity exposure), a portion of returns is tax-exempt under the _Teilfreistellung_ regime. For equity-oriented funds this exemption is 30%, effectively reducing the taxable portion of gains and distributions.

### Vorabpauschale (Advance Lump Sum Tax)

The Vorabpauschale is a notional advance tax on accumulating ETFs, designed to prevent investors from deferring all taxation until sale. It simulates a minimum annual return — as if the ETF had distributed dividends it never actually paid — and taxes that notional amount each year.

**Formula:**

```
Vorabpauschale = ETF value (first working day of January) × (Basiszins × 0.7) × (1 − Teilfreistellung rate)
```

Where **Basiszins** is the base rate published annually by the Deutsche Bundesbank. For example, if the Basiszins is 2.53%:

```
Vorabpauschale = ETF value × (2.53% × 0.7) × 0.7 (for equity ETFs)
```

**Key rules:**
- If the Basiszins is negative, no Vorabpauschale is owed
- If the ETF's value fell during the year, no Vorabpauschale is owed
- The Vorabpauschale is not double-taxed on sale: any amounts previously taxed are deducted from the capital gain at the time of sale. For example, if you paid €60 of Vorabpauschale over 30 years, those €60 reduce the taxable gain when you sell the ETF.

The Vorabpauschale is collected by the broker directly from the account at the start of each year. Ensure there is sufficient cash in the broker account in January to cover it.
